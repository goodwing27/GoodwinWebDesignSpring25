Penguins are Cool!

Introduction to Penguins

Penguins are my favorite animals because they are birds that cannot fly. There are 18 different species of penguins located across the coldest regions of the southern hemisphere! Penguins are colored black and white to camouflage themselves from aquatic predators in the water such as orcas and sharks. The most common types of penguins include emperor, king, macaroni, and chinstrap.

What Do Penguins Eat?

A penguins diet mainly consists of:
1. Fish
2. Squid
3. Krill
Between these three food sources it mostly depends on their species and habitat. They catch their food on daily dives into the freezing water. Though they are flightless birds, their wings/flippers do serve a very important purpose. They often come in handy when escaping predators. Due to overfishing, penguins are being challenged more than ever to swim further in the dangerous waters for food. It is important to remember that penguins are strictly carnivorous birds and don't ever consume plants so they MUST go on these dangerous dives for survival!

Breeding

In order to reproduce, penguins find a new mate each season. They attract their mates by exchanging pebbles as a gift! This is a special gift to penguins because they often use pebbles to construct a nest. Essentially, this action tells the penguin they want a family! Since penguins only lay 1-2 eggs per mating season, they must protect the eggs from land predators and keep them safe from the elements. That's why the father penguin stands on them in the snow. After enough eggs are laid a new colony is created. These penguins will spend much of the next year together. Once hatched, both parents work together in raising their baby penguins for a year. Then it is their turn!
