Penguins are Cool!

Introduction to Penguins

Penguins are my favorite animals because they are birds that cannot fly. There are 18 different species of penguins located across the coldest regions of the southern hemisphere! Penguins are colored black and white to camouflage themselves when are predators in the water such as orcas and sharks. The most common include Emperor, King, Macaroni, and Chinstrap.

What Do Penguins Eat?

Penguins mainly eat fish, squid, and krill. Between these three it mostly depends on their species and habitat. They catch their food on daily dives into the freezing water. Though they are flightless birds, their wings/flippers do serve a very important purpose. That purpose is to help them swim effectively through the water. It is important to remember that penguins are strictly carnivorous birds and don't ever consume plants.

A penguin swims through the frigid waters in search of its next meal!

Breeding

In order to reproduce, penguins find a new mate each season. They attract their mates by exchanging pebbles as a gift! This is a special gift to penguins because they often use pebbles to construct a nest. Essentially, this action tells the penguin they want a family! Once they have eggs they must protect and keep them warm. That's why the father penguin stands on them in the snow. Once hatched, both parents work together in raising their baby penguins for a year. Then it is their turn!
